Atmosphere

· Thin, primarily carbon dioxide.

· Possible carbon source for

chemosynthetic organisms.

Water Availability

· Scarce on the surface.

· Water-ice beneath the surface and in polar regions.

· Seasonal flows of briny water in craters create

potential habitats.

Surface Composition

· Iron-rich and oxidized, potentially

supporting iron-oxidizing bacteria.

similar to those on Earth.

Temperature Challenges

· Extremes can drop to -225°F (-153°C).

· Extremophiles could thrive in protected

subsurface environments.

Location

· An exoplanet about 600 light-years away in

the constellation Cygnus.

· Discovered in 2011 by NASA's Kepler Space

Telescope.

Habitable Zone

· First known planet orbiting within the

habitable zone of a sun-like star,

where liquid water could potentially exist.

Star Information

· Orbits Kepler-22, a slightly smaller and

cooler star than the Sun.

· Completes an orbit in about 290 days,

similar to Earth's orbit.

Physical Characteristics

· Radius 2.4 times larger than Earth,

classifying it as a super-Earth.

· Composition uncertain; may be rocky,

an ocean world, or gas-dominated.

Subsurface Ocean

· Thick ice shell covering a salty ocean,

potentially 40 to 100 miles deep.

· One of the most promising locations for

extraterrestrial life.

Recent Observations

· Detection of water vapor plumes suggests

ongoing geological processes.

Surface Features

· Primarily water ice with dark cracks and

minimal impact craters, indicating geological

activity.

Atmosphere and Magnetic Field

· Tenuous atmosphere contains oxygen.

· Magnetic field hints at a conductive ocean

beneath the ice, supporting the potential for life.
